Design language · Working draft · v1

Moldova agricultural flood-risk map

Before wireframes, a proposed visual system. The brief: read as institutional, not as a tech demo. Closer to a national mapping agency or a bank's risk portal than a SaaS landing page — but precise and modern enough to feel current. Built entirely on Radiant Earth's brand foundations (Vert Grotesk Display, Neue Swiss, Berkeley Mono; Ponderosa / Lagoon / Tangerine), adapted for a data instrument. Fathom's restraint — hairline rules, sharp corners, monospace labels, no decoration — is the reference for tone, not a template to copy.

01

Theme — light by default

Settled: the interface defaults to light — a clean, institutional register that reads like a public registry or a bank's risk report — with dark mode as an option, not a separate design. The map keeps a calm light basemap by default and lets the fields carry the colour. Throughout: straight hairline rules and sharp corners — zero rounded "app" buttons. Trust first, instrument second.

02

Palette — chrome is brand, data is its own language

A hard rule that keeps the map honest: Lagoon green is the UI / interaction colour (selection, active RP, links, buttons). It never appears in the flood ramp. Flood exposure gets its own blue ramp. Tangerine is reserved — one job only: it marks proposed capability (see §05). Keeping these three vocabularies separate is what lets a viewer always know whether they're looking at the interface, the evidence, or the pitch.

03

The flood-exposure ramp — the single most important colour decision

This ramp is the product. Two readings of "exposure" are possible and they imply different ramps — we should pick one (or support both as a toggle). Both shown on the dark map base they'll actually sit on.

Hydro — sequential blue

Locked
shallowdeep

Reads "depth of inundation at the selected return period." Water = blue is instantly legible to anyone; single-hue is colour-blind-safe and prints cleanly in reports. Calm, scientific, government-grade.

blue fields on a light basemap — hairline strokes keep parcels crisp

Severity — diverging to tangerine

Parked
floods rarelyfloods often

Reads "how frequently a field floods" — worst land (inundated even at RP10) glows in brand tangerine. More of an insurance/alarm register; ties to the brand accent. Trade-off: less intuitive, harder for colour-blind users.

tangerine = act-now parcels

05

Live vs. proposed — the system that lets the demo argue for its own replacement

Your core principle, made into a rule. Live = solid. Full-saturation fill, solid 1px stroke, a green DATE REALE stamp. Proposed = ghosted. Diagonal hatch, dashed tangerine stroke, a PROPUS stamp, and editable values shown in tangerine mono. A viewer can always tell evidence from pitch at a glance — no caption required.

Iconography — Lucide, 1.75px stroke

Line icons only, inheriting currentColor. No filled or coloured icons as decoration. A small functional set covers the whole tool.

For “fields” — chosen: the crop / sprout mark (far right, highlighted). Warmer and more legible at small sizes than the cadastral marks, and it reads instantly as agriculture.

08

Principles to hold through the wireframes

01

Three vocabularies, never blurred. Lagoon = interface. Blue ramp = evidence. Tangerine = proposal. If a colour is doing two jobs, it's wrong.

02

Solid is true, ghosted is proposed. The hatch/dash + stamp pair appears on every stub — map layers, panels, toggles — so the eye learns it once.

03

Provenance is always on screen, never apologetic. "Date reale · JRC 90m" sits in the chrome at all times; limits stated plainly, not hidden in a tooltip.

04

One big number. The headline stat earns the largest type on the page. Everything else is quieter than it.

05

The map is the hero; chrome recedes. Dark ground, hairline rules, mono labels. No gradients on chrome, no shadows-as-decoration, no rounded-pill SaaS look.
